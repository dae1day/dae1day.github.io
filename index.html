About Me


Joining the work industry from a young age, has led me to over 12 years of work experience mostly in the field of hospitality. Through the years and various roles I have learnt to work independently, in teams and as a leader. These experiences overall has molded my greatest traits, to be flexible and empathetic.

I graduated from the University of Technology, Sydney in 2016 with a bachelor of Arts in Communications, Information Media.

My hobbies include Taekwondo, starting at the age of 16, my achievements include 2008 National Champion, going into hiatus for study then later coming back to represent UTS for the 2013 Australian University Games winning a silver medal.

I took a gap year in 2015 travelling, dancing, and teaching english. An unplanned trip which ended being a 360 day trip, to 16 countries and 36 cities. In that year my passion for dance and photography grew. Interactions with people from different cultures also taught me the importance of communication is listening and understanding rather than speaking.

After graduation I continued to travel and work in hospitality, through a friends recommendation, I was introduced to Coder Academy where I am now currently studying at.
